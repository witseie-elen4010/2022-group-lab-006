Since 2022, The New York Times Company has owned and published Wordle, a web-based word game conceived and developed by Welsh software programmer Josh Wardle. Players get six chances to guess a five-letter word, with colored tiles showing when letters match or occupy the correct location providing feedback for each guess. The game's mechanics are substantially identical to those of Jotto, a 1955 pen-and-paper game, and Lingo, a television game show series. With all players competing to guess the same word, Wordle has a single daily solution.
